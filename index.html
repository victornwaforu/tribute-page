Dr. Mankombu Sambasivan Swaminathan

Father of Green Revolution in India

Dr. Mankombu Sambasivan Swaminathan in his unique botanical garden

Here's a time line of Dr. Swaminathan's life:

  • 1925 - Born in Kumbakonam, Tamil Nadu
  • 1940 - Went on to study Medicine which was natural since coming from a family of doctors. But he switched to zoology and decided to devote his life for getting rid of hunger from India when he witnessed the Great Bengal famine of 1943, and earned a Bachelor of Science degree in zoology.
  • 1947 - Moved to the Indian Agricultural Research Institute (IARI) in New Delhi as a post-graduate student in genetics and plant breeding.
  • 1949 - Obtained his post-graduate degree with high distinction in Cytogenetics.
  • 1950 - Moved to study at the Plant Breeding Institute of the University of Cambridge School of Agriculture.
  • 1952 - Earned a Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) degree for his thesis, "Species Differentiation, and the Nature of Polyploidy in certain species of the genus Solanum - section Tuberarium". His work presented a new concept of the species relationships within the tuber-bearing Solanum. And also accepted a post-doctoral research associateship at the University of Wisconsin, Laboratory of Genetics to help set up a USDA potato research station.
  • 1954 - Returns back to Homeland India.
  • 1955 - Marries Mina Swaminathan who he met while they were both studying at Cambridge. They have three daughters: Soumya Swaminathan, Madhura Swaminathan and Nitya Rao.
  • 1972 - Field research on Mexican dwarf wheat varieties. Teach Cytogenetics, Radiation Genetics, and Mutation Breeding and build up the wheat and rice germplasm collections at Indian Agricultural Research Institute IARI.
  • 1983 - Developed the concept of Farmers' Rights and the text of the International Undertaking on Plant Genetic Resources (IUPGR).President of the International Congress of Genetics.
  • 1993 - Founded and became the President, International Society for Mangrove Ecosystems (ISME)
  • 2002 - Co-chairman with Dr. Pedro Sanchezof the UN Millennium Task Force on Hunger, a comprehensive global action plan for fighting poverty, disease and environmental degradation in developing countries. And vver 68 students have done their Ph.D thesis work under his guidance.
  • 2006 - Was the featured speaker at The Norman E. Borlaug International Symposium: in Des Moines, Iowa on, October 19.
  • Currently - He holds the UNESCO -Cousteau Chair in Ecotechnology at the M. S. Swaminathan Research Foundation in Chennai, India and is also spearheading a movement to bridge the Digital divide called, “Mission 2007: Every Village a Knowledge Centre”.

"I prefer to share knowledge with women. If you do something for a woman the whole family benefits but if you do something for a man, the reverse is not true."

--- Prof. MS Swaminathan
